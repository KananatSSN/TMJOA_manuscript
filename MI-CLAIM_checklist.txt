- The clinical problem in which the model will be employed is clearly detailed in the paper.
The model will be used to classify Temporomandibular joint disorders namely condylar erosion, condyle flattening, generalize sclerosis, osteoarthritis, osteophyte and sub-condral cyst.

- The research question is clearly stated.
We investigate effectiveness of the 3D CNN based models on vexel data representation against 2D CNN based models with rule based layers of interest extraction method. The goal is to minimize the labeling effort required from the technician (in slice of interest selection step and the locating the sign of disorders on the image step) by training classification model and use grad cam to highlight the sign of disorder instead of training object detection model. The benefit of this approch are 2 folds, first is that it eliminate the need for bounding box labeling and second is that it also minimize the bias from labeler as the TMJ disorder are often ambiguous.

- Transformations of the data before it is applied to the proposed model are described.
1. Get the .dcm image
2. Convert to .nii.gz
3. Segment with DentalSegmentator
4. Crop the condyle head with our method
    4.1 Binarize the image
    4.2 Use the 3d skeletonization to skeletonize the image
    4.3 Get the skeleton point that is closest to a predefine landmark point
    4.4 Use the point from 4.3 as center, crop 254*254*254 box covering that point
5. For the data used for 2d model. The 2D images are extracted with 2 methods. 1. Extract from the slices around the slices with maximum non background pixel and 2. Render and extracted as multiview image.
